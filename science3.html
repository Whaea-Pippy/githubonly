Big Picture Science Timeline

Key moments in science history

Ancient Times – First Scientists

Thousands of years ago

People started asking “Why?” and “How?”

  • Ancient Greeks (Aristotle, ~350 BC) observed nature and asked questions
  • Early astronomers watched stars and planets
  • Inventions like the wheel and simple machines
  • Early medicine – learning how the body works
Science begins with curiosity – just like you in class!

1600s – The Scientific Revolution

1600–1700

Scientists start using experiments and evidence.

  • Galileo Galilei (1609) improves the telescope and studies space
  • Isaac Newton (1687) explains gravity and motion
  • First microscopes – discovering tiny cells
  • Scientific method begins to take shape
Evidence over guessing – the birth of modern science.

1700s – Age of Enlightenment

1700–1800

Science spreads and changes everyday life.

  • Electricity experiments (Benjamin Franklin, 1752)
  • Oxygen discovered (Joseph Priestley, 1774)
  • Steam engine improved – start of machines
  • Classification of living things begins (Linnaeus)
Science starts powering the world.

1800s – Huge Discoveries

1800–1900

Science explains life and energy.

  • Charles Darwin (1859) – theory of evolution
  • Cells discovered as building blocks of life (1839)
  • Electricity and magnetism linked (Faraday)
  • Periodic table created (Mendeleev, 1869)
We start understanding life, matter, and energy.

Early 1900s – New Ideas

1900–1950

Science changes how we see the universe.

  • Albert Einstein (1905, 1915) – relativity
  • Quantum theory – tiny particles behave strangely
  • Penicillin discovered (1928) – first antibiotic
  • DNA structure begins to be understood
The universe is weirder and more amazing than we thought!

1950–1980 – Space & DNA

1950–1980

Humans reach space and unlock life’s code.

  • DNA double helix discovered (Watson & Crick, 1953)
  • First human in space (Yuri Gagarin, 1961)
  • Moon landing (Apollo 11, 1969)
  • First computers and internet beginnings
We walk on the Moon and learn how life is built.

2000–2020 – Modern Science

2000–2020

Technology and discovery speed up.

  • Human Genome Project completed (2003)
  • Smartphones and internet everywhere
  • Higgs boson discovered (2012)
  • First image of a black hole (2019)
We map all human genes and see the invisible.

Today & The Future

2025 →

You are part of the next chapter!

  • Climate science drives action
  • Space exploration: Mars rovers, private rockets
  • AI and robotics changing daily life
  • Gene editing and new medicines
  • You asking questions and doing experiments
Science is not finished – it needs curious minds like yours!
What will you discover?

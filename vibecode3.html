Vibe Coding

What is Vibe Coding?

Vibe Coding is the practice of designing interactive learning experiences using clear instructions, pedagogy, and simple AI-assisted tools.

  • Flashcards, quizzes, matching, pre-reading activities
  • Reusable learning tools (not one-off worksheets)
  • Designed by teachers, for learners
Key idea: You design the learning flow — the technology supports it.

Why it matters (Years 7–10)

  • Transition years show the biggest engagement drop
  • Literacy demands increase across subjects
  • Students expect interaction, not passive content
Memory Engagement Confidence

Vibe Coding prepares students before they meet complex texts.

How it works in a lesson

Before reading a text

  • 6 key vocabulary flashcards
  • Quick matching game
  • 3-question prediction quiz
Less decoding. More comprehension. Better discussion.

Who benefits most?

  • Reluctant readers
  • Neurodiverse learners
  • ESOL / multilingual students
  • Students needing confidence to start
Low-stakes practice = high participation

Time to upskill (honest view)

  • Week 1: 30–45 minutes learning the workflow
  • Weeks 2–3: 1–2 hours experimenting
  • After that: Faster than worksheets
High return on time invested

Your first step

  • Choose one upcoming text
  • Identify 6 key words
  • Create flashcards + a short quiz
Start small. Reuse often. Build confidence.

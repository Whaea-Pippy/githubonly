AI in Education Worldwide

How countries are adopting AI tools like Vibe Coding (2025 snapshot)

New Zealand

Solid grassroots adoption – catching up on national strategy

  • 69% of teachers use AI weekly (mostly for planning, resources, and personalisation)
  • High individual innovation: teachers creating interactive tools independently
  • New high school curriculum adds AI and data literacy subjects
  • GenAI restricted in some national assessments, but encouraged for teaching
  • Calls for earlier AI education (before Year 13)
NZ strength: Teacher-led creativity (like Vibe Coding) is thriving despite slower national roll-out. Many educators are already ahead of policy.

Singapore

Global leader – systematic and ambitious

  • National AI Strategy 2.0 with heavy education investment
  • Student Learning Space integrates AI for personalised pathways
  • MOE AI-in-Education Ethics Framework guides responsible use
  • AI literacy built into curriculum; tools adapt to individual strengths
  • Focus on equity: AI to replicate small-class benefits at scale
Singapore shows what strong national vision + funding can achieve – personalised learning for every student.

Estonia

Bold national leap – AI tools for all

  • AI Leap 2025 programme: free advanced AI tools for every student and teacher
  • Custom Estonian-language AI models in classrooms
  • Focus on ethical use, digital competence, and meaningful learning
  • Building on decades of digital education leadership (e-Estonia)
  • Aiming for full integration by 2027
Estonia is setting the global standard: nationwide AI access + teacher support from day one.

Finland

Balanced and thoughtful integration

  • Strong focus on AI literacy and critical thinking (building on media literacy success)
  • National teacher training programmes for AI in education
  • Guidelines emphasise ethics, equity, and human-centred use
  • AI used to support differentiation and reduce teacher workload
  • Experimenting carefully – avoiding over-reliance
Finland prioritises understanding over tools – preparing students to use AI wisely.

China

Massive scale – compulsory and rapid

  • AI education compulsory in many regions (e.g. Hangzhou)
  • National goal: AI in all primary/secondary schools by 2030
  • Tiered curriculum from basic awareness to advanced projects
  • Huge investment in AI tutors, smart classrooms, and adaptive platforms
  • Focus on nurturing innovation and national competitiveness
China demonstrates speed and scale – reaching millions of students quickly.

United States

High usage – state-by-state variety

  • 85–86% of teachers and students used AI in 2024–25 school year
  • 28+ states have official AI guidance for K–12
  • Federal encouragement but implementation varies widely
  • Popular for personalised learning, admin tasks, and creative tools
  • Debates around equity, privacy, and assessment integrity
Like NZ, much US adoption is teacher-driven – innovation happens at classroom level.

Global View & Opportunity for NZ

NZ is not far behind – teacher adoption rates match or exceed many leaders.

  • Leaders (Singapore, Estonia, China) have strong national programmes and funding
  • NZ excels in creative, low-cost, teacher-led practices (perfect for Vibe Coding)
  • Grassroots innovation often spreads faster than top-down mandates
  • 2025–2026 curriculum changes will help close the policy gap
Your advantage: NZ teachers are already building reusable interactive resources with AI – share them, collaborate, and lead from the classroom.
